# Leopard — Product Vision

## What Leopard is

Leopard is a **reflection engine**. It turns your own data into understanding — locally, on your
own machine.

Leopard is **not** a coach, a scorekeeper, a replacement for raid leadership, an AI assistant, a
raid-analytics tool, or a replay viewer. It is a reflection of a particular *style* of leadership —
one that recognizes progress is rarely linear. Some bosses take time. Some nights require rest. Some
lessons take repetition before they become instinct.

## The real product

The product is a felt realization:

> *"I can ask questions of my own data, on my own machine, and trust the answer — because it's tied
> to what actually happened."*

Anyone can clone a chat box. Nobody can clone **grounded in YOUR data, on YOUR machine, yours
alone.** The differentiator is not Ask (a feature) — it's the **agency**: local, grounded, owned.
**Ask is the steering wheel; grounded local reflection is the engine.**

## What Leopard values

Momentum over perfection. It looks for resilience, recovery, adaptation, consistency, and growth.

Sometimes reflection means identifying a problem. Just as often, it means **recognizing improvement
that would otherwise go unnoticed.** The goal is never to tell players what to think — it is to help
them *see what is already present in the data* and start meaningful conversations around it.

## Raid data is the substrate, not the point

Raid data is the **familiar on-ramp** — gamers already learned to read combat logs because someone
taught them *why* logs matter. Nobody woke up one day wanting spreadsheets full of events.

The deeper mission is helping people build intuition for the chain:

> movement → events → logs → data structures → visualizations → insights → better questions

Leopard helps users walk that chain naturally. **This mission is the design compass — never the
pitch.** The instant Leopard *feels* like it is teaching, it dies. The teaching is a side effect of
the product being delightful at a concrete job: helping a team see what happened, what changed, and
what deserves attention.

## The surfaces — progressively richer reflections of one reality

- **Replay** provides evidence (place + time).
- **Shape** provides perspective (patterns across attempts).
- **Trends** provide context (signals over time).
- **Ask** provides exploration (interrogate it all, in your own words).

Together they transform information into understanding. Each surface can drill back down to the layer
beneath it — answer → trend → replay moment → events → log lines — so users *discover* the chain
silently, by delight rather than by lecture.

## The split

- **Tempo = Engine + Research + Lab.** The proven parser, analyzers, and contracts; the benches,
  Tuner, dual-B70, Intel/Vulkan, runtime-selection. The proving-ground. Also Leopard's **advanced**
  path.
- **Leopard = the Product.** Default path: **Leopard → local Ollama → an installed local model.** No
  specialized inference in the product surface; that lives in Tempo-advanced.

The user story:

> *You already have a gaming PC. You already have raid data. You already have a GPU. You can ask
> questions of your own data — locally.*

## Validated + sharpened in the field (2026-06-02)

Demoed to guild members in finance, accounting, data science, and engineering. They recognized
**Shapes** and **Trends** instantly — *"oh, clever"* — confirming the thesis (*make data not scary;
demystify + empower*) lands with the people who'd know a real one. That validated building Shapes +
Trends out, and parking Replay's player-display (the leader/guild lens).

It also surfaced the **flagship empowerment surface: the Pipeline Explorer** — the engine made
visible, the chain above turned into a clickable map with drill-in to real data subsets. It is the
purest expression of this mission, and Tempo's "make the math touchable" origin intent aimed at
users. See [feature-order.md](feature-order.md) and [pipeline-explorer.md](pipeline-explorer.md).
